Co-Occurring Conditions with ADHD

Living with ADHD can look different for everyone. Other conditions may appear alongside it, affecting how a person feels, learns, and manages daily life. Recognizing these co-occurring conditions is important, since they can influence how ADHD is experienced and treated.

Common Co-Occurring Conditions

Condition How It Relates to ADHD Common Symptoms Notes
Sleep Disorders ADHD can disrupt sleep cycles and cause irregular routines Insomnia, delayed sleep phase, fatigue Worsens focus, emotional regulation, and impulsivity
Anxiety Disorders Common due to chronic stress, perfectionism, and masking Excessive worry, restlessness, difficulty relaxing Can intensify inattention and overwhelm
Depression Can result from frustration, low self-esteem, or repeated setbacks Sadness, loss of motivation, fatigue ADHD in women can be misdiagnosed as depression due to overlapping symptoms
Learning Disabilities Often co-exists with attention difficulties, especially in reading or math Trouble processing written info or numbers Attention challenges may make learning difficulties more pronounced
Eating Disorders Impulsivity, emotional dysregulation, or body image concerns may contribute Restrictive eating, bingeing, or purging ADHD traits can make detection and management more difficult
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Shares overlapping traits like sensory sensitivity and social challenges Social difficulty, rigid routines, hyperfocus Social and executive function challenges can overlap, making support needs more complex
Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD) More common in children with impulsivity and frustration intolerance Irritability, defiance, frequent arguing Relatively uncommon in girls, may appear with frustration or masking
